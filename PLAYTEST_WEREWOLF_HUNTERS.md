# The White Fang Lie

## One-Hour NullFrame Playtest

### Playtest Goals

Use this scenario to test:

- `Settle` vs `Roll`
- Burdens reducing dice pools
- One-roll scene resolution
- Aspect creation and invocation
- PbtA-style spotlight flow
- Progress tracks
- Social pressure, investigation, and combat in one session
- At least one Mastery in play

## Premise

The hunters arrive in the village of `Greyhook` just before moonrise. Three villagers are dead. All signs point to a werewolf.

They were hired by the famous hunter `Harlan Voss`, who claims he already tracked the beast to the old mill in the woods.

The twist: `Harlan Voss` is the real killer.

He found an old wolfskin relic that gives him unnatural speed and strength. He has been using silver claws and staged attacks to flush out a hidden young werewolf so he can kill her publicly and become a legend.

The real werewolf, `Ada Rowan`, is a terrified miller's daughter who has hurt livestock and one hunter by accident, but she did not murder the villagers.

### Tone

Gothic hunt, cold rain, suspicion, lantern light, bad faith heroism.

## The Truth

These are the facts behind the mystery:

- `Ada Rowan` is the hidden werewolf
- Ada's father locked her in the mill cellar on full moons
- Ada has mauled animals and once wounded a hunter in panic
- She did **not** kill the three villagers
- `Harlan Voss` killed them
- Harlan used a wolfskin relic and silver claw gauntlet to fake beast attacks
- Harlan wants to kill Ada in public and take full credit

## Session Structure

### Scene 1: The Torn Farm

**Time:** 10 minutes

The hunters arrive at the latest kill site: a sheep farm outside Greyhook.

A dead farmhand lies in the mud. Villagers are angry, frightened, and ready to burn the woods.

Harlan is present. He is confident, charming, and trying to steer the party fast toward the mill.

#### Aspects

- `Blood-slick mud`
- `Terrified villagers`
- `Broken lantern light`
- `Fresh rain`
- `Silver wire under the body`

#### What This Scene Tests

- Investigation
- Social pressure
- Early burden use
- Whether players `Settle` or `Roll`
- Creating aspects from forensic details

#### Key Clues

- The wounds are partly torn, but some cuts are too clean
- There is silver wire near the body
- The corpse has defensive wounds against a human-sized attacker
- Harlan pushes too hard toward a quick hunt

#### Good Rolls Here

- Greta tracks the scene
- Sabine examines the body
- Ivo manages the crowd or reads Harlan
- Rook secures the site or finds planted gear

#### Good Null Moves

- A villager grabs a torch and wants blood now
- Rain starts erasing tracks
- Harlan "helpfully" points the party the wrong way
- The crowd starts blaming the Rowans

---

### Scene 2: The Black Pine Trail

**Time:** 10 to 12 minutes

The party follows signs into the woods.

This is a moving scene. Keep spotlight bouncing fast. Use one roll for bold actions.

#### Aspects

- `Thorn-choked path`
- `Fresh wolf spoor`
- `Hidden hunter markers`
- `Fallen pine across the trail`
- `Cold marsh water`

#### What This Scene Tests

- PbtA spotlight flow
- Tracking with Mastery
- Burdens in motion
- Group problem-solving
- Aspect use in a travel scene

#### What They Can Discover

- There are two trails, not one
- One trail is real wolf movement
- One trail is a human laying false signs
- Harlan's boot marks appear where they should not

#### Optional Pressure

Use a `Moonrise Clock` with 4 segments.

Advance it when:

- the hunters fail badly
- they waste time arguing
- they take loud or reckless actions
- they follow Harlan blindly

When full, Ada loses control in Scene 3 or Scene 4.

---

### Scene 3: The Rowan Chapel Cellar

**Time:** 12 to 15 minutes

The trail leads to an abandoned roadside chapel, then to a hidden cellar beneath it.

Ada is there, chained or half-freed depending on how fast the hunters moved.

Her father is dead or missing. If you want a sharper emotional hit, make him mortally wounded and barely able to explain the truth.

Ada is frightened, feverish, and on the edge of changing.

#### Aspects

- `Rusty chains`
- `Moonlight through broken stone`
- `Childhood drawings on the wall`
- `Old prayer symbols`
- `The smell of wet fur and incense`

#### What This Scene Tests

- Social conflict
- Burden pressure on Ivo
- Reading truth under stress
- Whether the party protects, cages, or executes Ada
- Creating advantages before the boss scene

#### What Ada Knows

- Harlan found her first
- He killed her father or attacked him
- He said he needed a monster to finish the story
- He is waiting at the mill for moonrise

#### If the Party Handles This Well

- Ada can become an ally, witness, or scene aspect in the finale

#### If They Handle This Badly

- Ada panics
- Mark the `Moonrise Clock`
- The cellar becomes a dangerous conflict scene instead of a revelation scene

---

### Scene 4: The Old Mill

**Time:** 18 to 20 minutes

This is the climax.

Harlan is at the mill with traps, silver chains, and a prepared story for the villagers. If exposed, he stops pretending and embraces the wolfskin relic.

He is not a true werewolf. He is a hunter who chose to become monstrous.

#### Aspects

- `Rotting mill beams`
- `Hanging chains`
- `Flammable grain dust`
- `Moonlit rafters`
- `Silver traps on the floor`

#### Boss Progress Track

`Harlan Voss` - 6 segments

Mark progress when the hunters:

- disarm him
- expose him publicly
- pin him in a trap
- break the relic's hold
- wound him cleanly
- force him into a bad position

#### Suggested Outcomes

- `Partial Success`: mark 1, but Harlan escalates or injures someone
- `Success`: mark 1 cleanly, or 2 with strong leverage
- `Critical Success`: mark 2 and create a major advantage

#### What This Scene Tests

- Progress tracks in a boss fight
- Created aspects paying off
- Environmental invocations
- Burdens under pressure
- Whether the hunters solve the problem by killing, exposing, trapping, or breaking the relic

#### Twist Payoff

If Ada is present, she can testify, help corner Harlan, or lose control and complicate the scene.

### Harlan Voss

**Public face:** charming legend, experienced hunter, speaks like he has already won  
**Real face:** vain, addicted to glory, believes monsters are useful if they make him famous

## Clean Ending Options

Use whichever ending the players earn.

### Ending 1: Hunters Kill Harlan

The village gets a simple answer. Ada's fate depends on what the hunters do next.

### Ending 2: Hunters Expose Harlan Publicly

Harder, but better. This tests whether social leverage can matter as much as violence.

### Ending 3: Hunters Kill Ada Too

A dark ending. Effective for tone, but morally ugly. Good for testing burdens and fallout.

### Ending 4: Hunters Spare Ada and Fake Her Death

Best if you want a strong story ending and a clear "hunter vs monster" reversal.

## Fast Start Script

Use this to open:

> Rain hammers the fences of Greyhook Farm.  
> A body lies in the mud beside a torn sheep pen.  
> Lanterns shake in frightened hands.  
> The villagers whisper one word over and over: `werewolf`.  
> Standing beside the corpse is Harlan Voss, the White Fang, smiling like the story is already his.  
> He turns as you arrive and says, `Good. We still have time to kill it before moonrise.`

## Null Notes For Testing

Push these on purpose during play:

- Offer at least one tempting `Settle` in every scene
- Make at least one burden obviously relevant for each hunter
- Put 3 to 5 clear aspects in every location
- Let social victories mark real progress, not just combat
- On failures, make hard moves that change the scene fast
- If pace slows, advance the `Moonrise Clock`
