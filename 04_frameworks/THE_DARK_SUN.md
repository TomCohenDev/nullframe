# Dark Sun Framework: Tyr

**Framework Status:** Draft Skeleton  
**World:** Athas  
**Primary Starting Location:** Tyr  
**Tone:** Brutal desert sword-and-sorcery / post-apocalyptic fantasy

---

## Framework Purpose

This framework adapts **Dark Sun** into NullFrame as a setting-specific layer focused on:

- survival in a dying world
- oppression within the city-state of Tyr
- wilderness danger beyond the city walls
- scarce resources and harsh choices
- magic and psionics as setting-defining forces
- character identity through race, path, proficiencies, specialties, and plays
- political sandbox in Tyr

This framework is intended as a **lightweight starting framework** for playtesting.

## Campaign Core

The story begins in and around **Tyr**.

At the center of the city stands the **great ziggurat**, built through massive labor and suffering. Publicly, it is a monument to the ruler’s will and glory. Secretly, it serves a deeper purpose connected to **King Kalak’s hidden plan to become a dragon**.

The players do **not** know this truth at the start.

Kalak is planning to sacrefice the ENTIRE city in order to get enough power.

### Starting Campaign Assumption

The players begin as people shaped by Tyr and Athas:
- survivors
- laborers
- escaped slaves
- arena fighters
- wasteland travelers
- agents of power
- scavengers
- outcasts
- hidden spellcasters
- psionic adepts

### Early Campaign Pressure

Use some combination of:
- ziggurat labor demands
- faction unrest
- missing people or materials
- growing fear in Tyr
- wilderness supply routes
- merchant schemes
- templar scrutiny
- strange magical or psionic signs

# Character Creation Structure

This framework uses the following structure for player characters.
## 1. Choose Race

Race gives identity, social pressure, and place in Athas.
## 2. Choose 1 Heritage or Background Proficiency

Choose **1** from the shared Athasian proficiency list below.

This represents upbringing, lived experience, culture, social position, or survival history.

## 3. Choose Path

Each Path provides:
- **2 Proficiency choices** from a list of 4
- **2 Specialty choices**
- **3 Plays chosen from a list of 5**

Path is the main gameplay identity of the character.

## 4. Choose 1 General Psionic Play

Choose **1** from the shared general psionics list below.

This reflects the setting assumption that psionic potential can appear across Athas, not only inside one race or one Path.

## 5. Write Core Character Elements

After choosing Race and Path, the player also defines:
- Burdens
- starting gear
- name / origin / social position
- 1 Heritage or Background Proficiency
- additional Proficiencies from path
- Specialties from path
- 1 General Psionic Play
- Plays from path

## 6. Edges in Play

**Edges keep their normal NullFrame meaning.**

They are not chosen at character creation as a separate build category.
Edges are still:
- favorable fictional factors
- gear, position, allies, preparation, information, environment, or weaknesses
- invoked during play for +1
Proficiencies and Specialties help justify Edges in play.

### Human

**Identity:** Adaptable, ambitious, and socially dominant across the Tablelands. Humans survive everywhere by persistence, daring, and flexibility. The most widespread people of Athas, found in the city-states, slave tribes, merchant dynasties, templarates, and the wastes.

**Athas Role:** The dominant people of the city-states and the broadest social category in Tyr, found everywhere from noble houses to caravans to slave lines.

**Notes:**
- Humans are the dominant people of the city-states and the most broadly adaptable race in Athas.
- Human appearance can vary widely, including subtle strange traits shaped by Athas.

### Mul

**Identity:** Bred for labour, battle, and endurance, muls are relentless, hard, and difficult to break. Common in slave pits, quarries, arenas, and military service; feared, exploited, and valued for sheer durability.

**Athas Role:** Living symbols of brutality, exploitation, and endurance; seen in Tyr as pit fighters, labor assets, guards, rebels, and survivors.

**Notes:**
- Muls are sterile and usually born through brutal slave-breeding programs.
- They are physically imposing but socially marked by slavery, ownership, and exploitation.

### Elf

**Identity:** Swift desert runners, traders, raiders, liars, and survivors. Elves live in motion and thrive in open land.  Known throughout Athas as runners, scouts, raiders, traders, and opportunists; trusted mainly within their own tribes.

**Athas Role:** Tribal runners, scouts, traders, raiders, and smugglers who move between city and wasteland more freely than most people ever can.

**Notes:**
- Elves are intensely tribal and distrust outsiders, even other elves from other tribes.
- They prize speed, freedom, and immediate advantage.

### Half-Elf

**Identity:** Caught between worlds and rarely fully accepted by either, half-elves survive through adaptability, caution, and reading people fast. They often live as messengers, scouts, intermediaries, drifters, or outsiders who have learned not to rely on belonging.

**Athas Role:** Useful go-betweens, suspect outsiders, and practical survivors who can move through several social spaces without truly being claimed by any of them.

**Notes:**
- Half-elves are often treated as untrustworthy, rootless, or available to be used by stronger powers.
- Their social position in Athas often rewards flexibility, observation, and emotional self-protection.

### Dwarf

**Identity:** Serious, driven, and defined by purpose. Dwarves are relentless when they commit themselves to a task.  Labourers, smiths, mercenaries, traders, and stubborn allies or enemies whose commitments run deeper than convenience.

**Athas Role:** Respected for stubborn reliability and feared for the same trait when their focus turns against someone; common as workers, crafters, traders, and hard allies.

**Notes:**
- Dwarven life is shaped by focus: a chosen purpose that defines effort and identity.
- Dwarves are deeply communal but highly private about what matters most to them.

### Half-Giant

**Identity:** Massive, imitative, and overwhelming. Half-giants combine terrible strength with social uncertainty and shifting identity.  Labourers, guards, soldiers, gladiators, raiders, and cultural imitators shaped by whoever currently leads them.

**Athas Role:** Huge laborers, enforcers, soldiers, and arena monsters whose presence changes every scene even before they speak.

**Notes:**

- Half-giants are famous for size, social awkwardness, and being shaped by nearby stronger personalities.
- Everything around them is harder to fit, carry, buy, or avoid breaking.

### Thri-kreen

**Identity:** Tireless insect hunters who live for the hunt, move fast, need little, and think in terms of clutch and pack.  
**Athas Role:** Feared wasteland hunters, strange but formidable allies, and relentless trackers who never truly rest.

**Notes:**

- Thri-kreen do not think like humanoids and tend to interpret social life through dominance, hunting, and pack bonds.
- They require little water and no sleep, which changes how they move through the world.

### Halfling

**Identity:** Fierce, agile, deeply rooted in ancient jungle culture, and often baffled or disgusted by the Tablelands.  
**Athas Role:** Rare outsiders from the Forest Ridge; scouts, hunters, fierce traditionalists, and protectors of the natural world.

**Notes:**

- Halflings are culturally insular, strongly anti-slavery, and often have trouble understanding city customs.
- They are small, fast, and far more dangerous than many outsiders assume.

### Pterran
**Identity:** Reptilian children of the Earth Mother, earnest, ceremonial, and guided by chosen life paths.  
**Athas Role:** Rare travellers from beyond the Ringing Mountains; spiritual wanderers, defenders of nature, and cautious observers of the Tablelands.

**Notes:**

- Pterrans are shaped by ritual, gratitude, and devotion to Athas as the Earth Mother.
- In the source material, the Path of the Mind is viewed with some suspicion even within pterran society.

### Aarakocra

**Identity:** Mountain-dwelling bird-people of freedom, height, and distance, uneasy in confinement and protective of their own space.  
**Athas Role:** Rare outsiders from aeries and mountain tribes; scouts, raiders, toll-keepers, and sky-watchers seldom comfortable in cities.

**Notes:**
- Aarakocra are intensely freedom-loving and often claustrophobic.
- They are often suspicious of lowland politics and prefer distance, altitude, and clear air.

## Shared Heritage and Background Proficiencies

Every player character chooses **1** of the following or create their own, regardless of race.

These are intentionally about upbringing, role, survival history, and social position in Athas rather than biology.

**Urban & Social**
- **City-State Underbelly**: I survived the cutthroat alleys, black markets, and political traps of the city.
- **Power Player**: I've navigated the lethal etiquette of courts, temples, and the sorcerer-king's inner circle.
- **Kept Servant**: I served in a noble house or temple, close enough to power to learn its habits and its cruelties.

**Oppression & Endurance**
- **Broken Chain Survivor**: I endured the slave-pits, ziggurat labor, and the lash of the overseer.
- **Hidden Caster**: I've concealed my arcane study from templars, mobs, and my own family.

**Wilderness & Survival**
- **Wasteland Nomad**: I know the hidden springs, safest paths, and the art of crossing the shimmering wastes.
- **Apex Predator**: I've stalked and killed the most dangerous beasts of Athas for meat, hide, and glory.
- **Highland Survivor**: I've mastered the unique dangers of the Forest Ridges or the high mountain peaks.

**Trade & Craft**
- **Scavenger-Crafter**: I've forged tools from obsidian and bone and salvaged value from the ruins of the Old World.
- **Caravan Broker**: I've managed the logistics of scarcity, bargaining and leading convoys through the dunes.

**Culture & Lore**
- **Keeper of the Old Ways**: I've learned the oral histories, forbidden lore, and buried truths that others have forgotten.

## General Psionic Plays

Every player character may choose **1** of the following, regardless of race.

These are meant to be broad wild talents, not full Path-defining powers.

**Finding & Sensing**
- **Water Sense** — Once per scene, extend your mind and feel the nearest source of drinkable water within roughly a day's travel. You know the direction and rough distance, not the quality or who guards it.
- **Life Echo** — Once per scene, sense the nearest living minds in a small area. You learn their rough number, rough size, and whether they are aware of you. No details, no identities.
- **Danger Prickle** — Once per scene, receive a warning pulse the moment before a hidden threat acts — an ambush, a trap, a predator's charge. You don't know _what_, only _now_ and _from there_.
- **Stone Memory** — Once per scene, touch worked stone, bone, or metal and catch a fragment of its history: who made it, who last held it, or what violence it witnessed. Fragmentary, never complete.

**Enduring & Persisting**
- **Drive Through Pain** — Once per scene, burn your mind's reserves to ignore fatigue, thirst, injury, or strain for one meaningful action. Afterward, the cost returns doubled — you collapse harder when the scene ends.
- **Heat-Shed** — Once per scene, flush the killing heat of Athas out of your body for a short stretch. You and one person touching you can walk through conditions that would otherwise drop you.
- **Stubborn Mind** — Once per scene, shut out fear, manipulation, despair, or a hostile mental intrusion. While it holds, nothing external can shake your resolve.

**Reaching & Coordinating**
- **Silent Word** — Once per scene, send a brief, wordless impression to an ally you can see — a warning, an image, a feeling. No full sentences; just a pulse of meaning.
- **Fleet Thought** — Once per scene, act first in a sudden moment of movement, escape, or pursuit. Your nervous system fires before it should.

**Finding the Truth**
- **Read the Air** — Once per scene, feel the emotional temperature of a group or scene. You learn what the dominant feeling is (fear, greed, suspicion, hope) and roughly who is driving it. You don't learn _why_.

# Paths

## Path Purpose

Paths are the main build identity for the framework.
A Path is a **general set of Plays** a player can choose from. It should also shape:
- the character’s main role in the setting
- likely Proficiencies
- likely Specialties
- what kind of scenes the character shines in

Paths should feel native to **Tyr and Athas**, not generic fantasy.

---

## Path Families and Subpaths

For this framework, a player chooses a **subpath** as their actual Path.

The larger grouping is only there to organize the Dark Sun class space.

### Warrior Paths

- **Fighter** — disciplined weapon master, soldier, mercenary, or hardened bodyguard
- **Gladiator** — arena killer, crowd fighter, show warrior, or escaped pit champion
- **Ranger** — wasteland hunter, scout, tracker, and survivor of the deep desert

### Arcane Paths

- **Defiler** — arcane caster who draws power with no regard for the land
- **Preserver** — arcane caster who works carefully to avoid ravaging the world
- **Illusionist** — subtle arcane deceiver focused on misdirection, concealment, and false appearances

### Priest Paths

- **Cleric** — servant of elemental power and the harsh forces that still answer prayer on Athas
- **Templar** — agent of a sorcerer-king, empowered through authority, fear, and political hierarchy
- **Druid** — protector of a place, spring, hidden grove, or living region of Athas

### Rogue Paths

- **Thief** — infiltrator, cutpurse, scout, burglar, and underworld operator
- **Bard** — poisoner, manipulator, performer, spy, and social predator
- **Trader** — caravan operator, fixer, broker, smuggler, and master of scarcity deals

### Exclusions and Scope Notes

- **Paladin** has no place in Athas and is intentionally excluded.
- **General psionics** are available to everyone through the shared psionic play list.
- A dedicated **Psion** subpath can be added later if playtesting shows that general psionics are not enough.

## Path: Gladiator

**Path Family:** Warrior **Role:** Living entertainment of the sorcerer-kings — pit-bred killers whose violence is spectacle. **Core Fantasy:** You don't just fight. You perform.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I studied the arena weapons schools.** I know the techniques, stances, and signature moves of trained gladiator combat.
- **I learned to fight for a crowd.** I know showmanship, timing, how to sell a blow, and how to shape a fight into a story.
- **I was taught to turn a broken blade into a kill.** I know improvised weapons, battlefield scavenging, and making any object into a tool of violence.
- **I mastered the one-on-one duel.** I know single combat — reading stance, pressing openings, and the pacing of a contest between two killers.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Signature Arena Weapon** — pick one (gythka, trikal, cahulak, net-and-dagger, etc.). +1 with that weapon.
- **Dirty Tricks** — +1 on sand, low kicks, grapples, bites, throat shots, crowd-pleasing foul play.
- **Crowd Reading** — +1 reading a hostile group, intimidating onlookers, provoking a reaction, playing an audience.
- **Opening Strike** — +1 on the first exchange of a fight, from a gate, a called challenge, or an ambush's first breath.

### Plays (choose 3 of 5)

- **Scars as Armor** _(signature)_ — You ignore the first -1 Bain invoked against you in every scene.
- **Showstopper** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, declare a theatrical action. Gain an Edge for audacity; on Partial Success or worse, the consequence is equally dramatic.
- **Weapon in Every Hand** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, declare an improvised weapon from the environment. Treat it as if you had Proficiency with it, invoke as an Edge on first attack.
- **Know Your Opponent** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, watch an enemy for one beat. Learn one weakness, Burden, habit, or fear. Becomes an Edge against them for the scene.
- **Crowd Turn** _(rare, once per session)_ — In a scene with onlookers, turn the crowd's attention and mood toward a target you name.

## Path: Fighter

**Path Family:** Warrior **Role:** Disciplined soldier, mercenary, bodyguard, or city-state militia. **Core Fantasy:** You don't rely on tricks or spectacle. You rely on training, position, and doing the job right.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I trained with the regular military.** I know drill, formation, chain of command, and how armies actually work.
- **I learned the weapon disciplines.** I know the formal schools of blade, spear, bow, or polearm — stance, grip, and the proven forms.
- **I served as a bodyguard or personal retainer.** I know close-protection work, reading threats in a room, and dying for someone else if it comes to that.
- **I led men into fights and brought most of them back.** I know small-unit tactics, terrain use, and keeping people alive when things go wrong.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Signature Weapon Discipline** — pick one (sword, spear, bow, polearm, etc.). +1 with that weapon.
- **Defensive Form** — +1 when holding ground, protecting an ally, or absorbing a charge.
- **Unit Tactics** — +1 when coordinating with allies, flanking, or positioning in a group fight.
- **Threat Assessment** — +1 when sizing up an opponent or room before violence starts.

### Plays (choose 3 of 5)

- **Drilled Instinct** _(signature)_ — You never lose access to a weapon-based Edge from fatigue, surprise, or panic. Training holds when nothing else does.
- **Covering Blow** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, spend your action to protect an ally. They gain an Edge on their next defense; any Bain aimed at them redirects to you instead.
- **Read the Field** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, before combat starts, identify the most dangerous enemy and the best piece of terrain. Each becomes an Edge you can invoke.
- **Hold the Line** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, plant yourself in a position. Until you move, no enemy can advance past you without creating an Edge for your allies.
- **Veteran's Gambit** _(rare, once per session)_ — Invoke a prior battlefield you survived. Name something you learned there, and treat it as a Specialty for the rest of the scene.

## Path: Ranger

**Path Family:** Warrior **Role:** Wasteland hunter, scout, tracker, survivor of the deep desert. **Core Fantasy:** The wastes are yours. You read them like a book, and the things that hunt others hunt beside you.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I learned to track across the wastes.** I know prints, sign, spoor, and reading ground that others say has nothing to say.
- **I trained to move unseen in open country.** I know concealment in sparse terrain, silhouette discipline, and stalking prey that has no cover either.
- **I studied the beasts and predators of Athas.** I know their habits, their weaknesses, their territories, and what they're afraid of.
- **I bonded with an animal of the wastes.** I know handling, training, and working alongside a creature that trusts me.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Signature Weapon** — pick one (bow, spear, thrown blade, etc.). +1 with that weapon.
- **One Named Beast** — pick one Athasian creature (tembo, kirre, silt horror, braxat). +1 against or tracking that specific thing.
- **Ambush** — +1 when attacking from cover or striking before the enemy knows you're there.
- **Wasteland Movement** — +1 on long pursuits, silent travel, and moving across dunes, ridges, or broken ground.

### Plays (choose 3 of 5)

- **Marked Quarry** _(signature)_ — Once you've tracked a specific creature or person for a full scene, they cannot lose you except through fictional means (a river, a psionic mask, a specific prepared trick).
- **First Shot** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, if you act before the enemy knows you're there, you get two Edges on your opening action instead of one.
- **Land Speaks** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, learn something about the immediate area from the land itself — recent passersby, a water source, a hidden threat, a safe route.
- **Beast at My Side** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, your bonded animal acts independently to create an Edge, remove a Bain, or distract an enemy.
- **Vanish Into the Waste** _(rare, once per session)_ — Once per session, if there's any plausible cover — a dune, a ridge, a dust-devil, an overhang — you and anyone holding your hand disappear from pursuit. The pursuers cannot find you again this scene.

## Path: Bard

**Path Family:** Rogue **Role:** Poisoner, performer, information broker, social predator — the dark-alley intellectual of the Tyr region. **Core Fantasy:** You are the most dangerous person in any room, and most people don't realize it until the poison takes hold.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I trained in the Bard's Quarter.** I know poisons, antidotes, alchemy, and the underground markets where they change hands.
- **I mastered the performer's arts.** I know music, storytelling, dance, acting, and holding a crowd in the palm of my hand.
- **I learned to trade in secrets.** I know information brokering, eavesdropping, and the real price of a name whispered in the right ear.
- **I studied the subtle weapons.** I know garrotes, hidden blades, needles, and killing someone without it looking like murder.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Signature Poison** — pick one (contact, ingested, dart-delivered, psychoactive). +1 when deploying that type.
- **Performance** — pick a form (music, oration, dance, storytelling). +1 when using it to hold, sway, or distract.
- **Reading People** — +1 when judging someone's motive, honesty, fear, or pressure points in conversation.
- **Misdirection** — +1 when drawing attention away from what actually matters — a heist, an escape, a poisoning.

### Plays (choose 3 of 5)

- **Always Listening** _(signature)_ — In any settled scene — a market, a tavern, a court, a caravan camp — you may declare one piece of information you overheard. The Null must make it true and useful, though it may be incomplete or dangerous to act on.
- **Venom in the Cup** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, reveal that you poisoned something earlier — a drink, a weapon, a doorframe. The poison takes effect now, and the target discovers it only when it matters.
- **Hold the Room** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, perform for an audience. For as long as you perform, no hostile action occurs — not because violence is impossible, but because everyone wants to see how the performance ends. You can't act otherwise while holding the room.
- **Open Secret** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, reveal a true thing about someone — a hidden shame, a lie they told, a debt they owe — in front of people who didn't know. The revelation becomes a Bain against them for the rest of the scene.
- **The Last Performance** _(rare, once per session)_ — Once per session, stage something — an assassination made to look like an accident, a heist disguised as a show, a rumor that topples a house. Work out the setup with the Null. Execution becomes a single roll with three Edges.

## Path: Trader

**Path Family:** Rogue **Role:** Caravan operator, merchant-house fixer, smuggler, and master of deals in a world where scarcity is life and death. **Core Fantasy:** Everything has a price. You know what it is, who pays it, and how to be the one collecting.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I ran caravans across the wastes.** I know routes, supply, convoy defense, and keeping a dozen people alive for weeks in the open desert.
- **I trained under a merchant house.** I know the great houses, their rivalries, their rates, and how to move goods legally and otherwise.
- **I learned to bargain anything against anything.** I know negotiation, contract, leverage, and the value of a thing to the specific person in front of me.
- **I ran contraband.** I know smuggling — hidden compartments, bribing guards, disguising cargo, and moving what isn't supposed to move.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Signature Cargo** — pick one (spice, silk, obsidian, slaves, water, arcane goods). +1 when dealing in that commodity.
- **Appraisal** — +1 when judging the true value of an object, document, or person's offer.
- **The Hard Sell** — +1 when bargaining from a weak position — cornered, outnumbered, or with the buyer's knife out.
- **Route Knowledge** — +1 when navigating trade paths, finding contacts in a city, or knowing which well is safe.

### Plays (choose 3 of 5)

- **Contacts Everywhere** _(signature)_ — In any city-state, outpost, or merchant caravan, you may declare that you know someone here. Name the kind of person (a fence, an ex-partner, a rival's debtor) and they exist. The relationship is whatever the Null decides it is.
- **The Right Gear** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, produce a small, plausible item from your kit — a vial, a tool, a document, a coin pouch, a weapon — that you didn't declare before. It must fit what a trader could reasonably carry.
- **Cut a Deal** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, in the middle of a standoff or negotiation, propose a deal that gives every party something they want. The Null determines the price, but the deal holds — for now.
- **Reading the Market** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, ask the Null what someone in the scene _wants_ most right now — not their stated goal, but their real need. Use that as an Edge in any interaction with them.
- **Call in the Caravan** _(rare, once per session)_ — Once per session, your merchant connections arrive — a supply drop, a rescue wagon, reinforcements, a bribe delivered at exactly the right moment. The Null decides the form. You owe someone afterward.


## Path: Thief

**Path Family:** Rogue **Role:** Infiltrator, cutpurse, scout, burglar, and underworld operator — the quiet profession in a city of loud cruelties. **Core Fantasy:** Locks open for you. Walls have paths. Guards have rhythms. The city is a machine, and you know how to slip between its gears.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I learned the underworld trade.** I know fences, safe houses, signs, and the unwritten rules of thieves working the same city.
- **I trained in infiltration.** I know locks, traps, alarms, and moving through a building designed to stop people like me.
- **I mastered silent movement.** I know rooftops, alleys, shadows, and footing — moving past guards who are looking right at me.
- **I studied the scout's craft.** I know lookout work, scouting routes, reading a room before entering, and getting out clean.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Signature Lockwork** — pick one (mechanical locks, magical seals, wards, knots). +1 when defeating that type.
- **Pickpocket** — +1 when lifting, palming, or planting something on a person.
- **Climb** — +1 on walls, rooftops, cliffs, and anything that wasn't meant to be climbed.
- **Blade in the Back** — +1 on attacks against a target who doesn't know you're there.

### Plays (choose 3 of 5)

- **Always a Way Out** _(signature)_ — In any enclosed space — a room, a building, a plaza, a dungeon — you may declare an escape route. The Null sets the complication (a guard, a drop, a crawl), but the route exists.
- **Slip the Eye** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, vanish from sight in a busy or cluttered scene. Guards lose you, eyes slide off you, you're gone. Holds until you act overtly again.
- **Crack the Lock** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, open a locked or sealed thing — a door, a chest, a window, a ward. The Null may add a complication (time, noise, a mark left behind), but the barrier opens.
- **One Step Ahead** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, reveal that you anticipated this — a rope prepared, a bribe paid earlier, a false trail laid. Narrate it, invoke it as an Edge.
- **The Perfect Job** _(rare, once per session)_ — Once per session, execute a planned theft, infiltration, or escape as a single montage. Work out the target and the crew with the Null. Resolve with one roll, three Edges. Success means clean; Partial means complicated; Failure means the job becomes the scene.
## Path: Defiler

**Path Family:** Arcane **Role:** Student of the forbidden arts in a world that kills wizards on sight. Whatever you actually do, the people around you call you a defiler. **Core Fantasy:** You know what magic was before the Cleansing, and what it could still be. Every cast is a choice: take the slow road and hide, or take the fast road and leave a scar. **Energy Source:** Plants by default. Animals with specific training. Self through sacrifice.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I studied the arcane arts in secret.** I know spell theory, arcane symbols, ritual structure, and the hidden schools.
- **I learned to hide what I am.** I know spellcasting disguise — muffling gestures, casting under the table, and looking like nothing at all.
- **I've read the old green-age texts.** I know what magic was before the Cleansing, and what it could be again.
- **I trained in drawing from the living world.** I know how life energy moves, what gives it willingly and what doesn't, and how to take from things other than plants.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Signature School** — pick one: Fire, Force, Transmutation, Divination, Protection. +1 when casting in that school.
- **Patient Casting** — +1 on preserved casts with time to prepare — minutes or hours, not seconds.
- **Battle Casting** — +1 on defiled casts under duress — combat, pursuit, interruption.
- **Arcane Lore** — +1 when identifying a spell, ward, or arcane effect, or reading spellbooks and scrolls.

### Plays (all 5)

- **Cantrip Work** _(signature)_ — You can always produce a minor arcane effect — light, tremor, heat, cold, a small illusion, a flicker of force — as a free Edge once per scene. Requires no formal casting, no source drawn. It's the muscle memory of someone who has lived with magic.
- **Cast the Shape** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, shape the air, ground, or objects in a scene — move a thing, lift a person, bend a blade, throw a shove. Treat as a spell. The form is yours to name.
- **Cast the Flame** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, call raw destructive force — fire, shockwave, shattering cold, arcane bolt — against a target or area. Treat as a spell.
- **Read the Weave** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, sense active magic within sight — identify other casters, wards, and spell effects. You know what you're looking at, and sometimes who cast it. This Play cannot be defiled; it's pure perception.
- **Unmaking** _(rare, once per session)_ — Once per session, cast a spell of catastrophic scope — level a structure, boil a well, kill a field of enemies, unseal an ancient working. The Null helps you define the shape. If defiled, the surrounding land dies out to a hundred paces and will not recover for generations.

### How Defile & Preserve modify these Plays

Every magical Play above (all except **Read the Weave** and **Cantrip Work**) can be cast in either mode:

- **Preserved:** Requires a beat of setup and a living source within reach. Resolves normally. Leaves no trace.
- **Defiled:** Casts immediately. Gain one automatic Edge. Works even in dead land. Leaves a visible blight scaled to the Play. Marks you for the next scene in any settled place.

## Path: Cleric

**Path Family:** Priest **Role:** Servant of an elemental force — earth, air, fire, water. Where your element is, you are strong. Where it is absent, you are nothing. **Core Fantasy:** You answer to something older and crueler than any king. When it answers back, the world bends. **Energy Source:** Your chosen element. You cannot draw from life at all — which means no plant is in danger from you, but also that a fire cleric in a rainstorm has nothing to work with.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I was trained in elemental ritual.** I know the prayers, invocations, and ceremonies that call the element's attention.
- **I studied the elemental lore.** I know how the elements and paraelements relate, what they want, and what they hate.
- **I served as a wandering priest.** I know the people of oases, villages, and slave tribes — and the needs that bring them to an altar.
- **I learned the healing arts of my element.** I know how my element mends, sustains, or punishes the living body.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Signature Element** — pick one: Earth, Air, Fire, Water. +1 when drawing from that element.
- **Healing Touch** — +1 when treating wounds, exhaustion, poison, or suffering.
- **Elemental Ritual** — +1 when performing ceremonies, communing with the element, or interpreting signs.
- **Command Presence** — +1 when leading a crowd in prayer, invoking authority by rite, or facing down supernatural threats.

### Plays (all 5)

- **The Element Answers** _(signature)_ — When you are near a significant manifestation of your element (a fire, a spring, open sky, bare stone), you may always invoke it as an Edge. In its absence, your magic is harder or impossible.
- **Call the Element** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, summon a brief manifestation of your element — a gust, a flame, a tremor, a splash of water — shaped to help or harm. Treat as a spell.
- **Lay On Hands** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, remove a Bain from yourself or an ally through direct elemental contact. The touch leaves a visible mark (scorched, chilled, cracked, damp).
- **Speak for the Element** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, interpret a sign, omen, or natural phenomenon on behalf of your element. The Null tells you one true thing the element "knows" about the scene. This Play cannot be defiled.
- **Wrath of the Element** _(rare, once per session)_ — Once per session, call down your element's full fury — a geyser, a firestorm, a collapsing earth, a killing wind. Scene-scale destruction shaped toward whatever you named.

### How Defile & Preserve modify these Plays

A Cleric defiles by **taking from their element more greedily than it can replenish.** A fire cleric who defiles a campfire: the fire dies, the wood turns to cold ash, and the land around cools unnaturally for hours. A water cleric who defiles a spring: the spring runs dry and will not return. An air cleric who defiles: the wind dies in a perfect dome of stillness that suffocates anything small inside it.

- **Preserved:** Beat of setup, element present in meaningful form. Resolves normally.
- **Defiled:** Immediate. Gain one Edge. The element withdraws from the place you defiled — a fire cleric's fires gutter near them for the rest of the scene, a water cleric's springs dry up, etc. Public defiling damages the cleric's standing with their temple if anyone loyal sees it.

If a Cleric is recognized as serving their temple, their public standing is **priest**, not defiler, regardless of how they cast. The moment they're cut off from their temple, they become a defiler in the street's eyes — even if every cast is preserved.

## Path: Druid

**Path Family:** Priest **Role:** Protector of a specific piece of Athas — a spring, a grove, a ridge, a hidden valley. A loner, an assassin, a guardian. **Core Fantasy:** You are the land's vengeance. What you protect, nothing takes without paying. **Energy Source:** Living things, with a covenant. A druid preserves almost always; to defile is to betray the thing you're sworn to protect.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I bonded with a guarded place.** I know every stone, spring, creature, and footprint in my territory.
- **I learned the ways of the land spirits.** I know which small spirits inhabit which features, what they remember, and how to ask them for help.
- **I trained in the old wild disciplines.** I know how to move unseen, strike from ambush, and vanish into terrain that others can't read.
- **I studied the balance of Athas.** I know the cycles of predator and prey, growth and decay, and how defiling breaks them.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Guarded Place** — name one specific location. +1 on any action taken within it.
- **Wild Shape** — pick one animal form you can assume. +1 when in or using that form.
- **Tracking** — +1 when following a creature or person across the land you know.
- **Poisonous Flora** — +1 when preparing, identifying, or resisting plant-based toxins and remedies.

### Plays (all 5)

- **Land Remembers** _(signature)_ — Within your guarded place, the land itself acts as an ally. You always have fictional support for movement, concealment, and information about anyone who has been there recently.
- **Shape of the Beast** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, take the form of your chosen animal. You move, sense, and act as it for one meaningful action — a flight, a hunt, a silent approach — then return to your own shape.
- **Call the Small Spirits** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, wake a minor spirit in a stone, tree, spring, or cactus. It does one thing for you — warn, distract, impede, witness — before settling back to sleep.
- **Hunter's Mark** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, mark a creature or person who harmed your guarded place. Until the scene ends, you always know their direction and distance, and they cannot surprise you. This Play cannot be defiled.
- **Wrath of the Land** _(rare, once per session)_ — Once per session, within or near your guarded place, unleash the land's defense — roots, stones, beasts, weather. Scene-scale.

### How Defile & Preserve modify these Plays

A Druid's magic is **preserved by default.** Their covenant with a guarded place means they draw energy gently, and the land consents.

- **Preserved:** Resolves normally. The land is willing.
- **Defiled:** Available, but a Druid who defiles **breaks their bond with their guarded place for the rest of the session.** Land Remembers and Wrath of the Land cannot be used until the bond is renewed through fiction — ritual, atonement, or a season of stewardship. Defiling outside their guarded place is less catastrophic but still marks them. A Druid who defiles habitually stops being a Druid and becomes something else.

A Druid who still defends a recognized sacred place is a **protector** in the street's eyes. A Druid who wanders or whose place has been destroyed is a defiler, regardless of how they cast.

## Path: Templar

**Path Family:** Priest **Role:** Agent of a sorcerer-king — bureaucrat, inquisitor, enforcer. Your power doesn't come from faith. It comes from a warrant. **Core Fantasy:** You don't pray. You don't draw from the land. You invoke your king's authority, and the king pays the cost somewhere you can't see. **Energy Source:** The sorcerer-king's power. The king drew it from somewhere — usually sentient life — long ago or far away, and holds it in reserve for agents like you. You leave no local blight. But every cast adds to a debt the king collects elsewhere.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I was trained in the templar bureaucracy.** I know ranks, protocols, writs, and how to make the machinery of a city-state do what I want.
- **I learned to channel my sorcerer-king's power.** I know the rituals of invocation, the price of the pact, and what the king expects in return.
- **I served as an inquisitor or enforcer.** I know interrogation, intimidation, arrest procedure, and how fear is applied by the state.
- **I navigated the templar court.** I know the politics, rivalries, and poisons of the sorcerer-king's inner circle.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Signature Domain** — pick one: Fire, War, Decrees, Torment, Surveillance. +1 when invoking that domain's magic.
- **Warrant** — +1 when invoking your legal authority — arrests, commanding guards, demanding entry.
- **Interrogation** — +1 when questioning a prisoner, witness, or suspect under pressure.
- **Court Politics** — +1 when maneuvering within templar or noble hierarchies.

### Plays (all 5)

- **By the King's Will** _(signature)_ — In any settlement loyal to your sorcerer-king, the fiction always supports you having legal authority over lesser citizens. Guards obey, doors open, subjects kneel — unless something specifically overrides it.
- **Channel the King** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, invoke your sorcerer-king's power for a direct arcane effect — fear, flame, compulsion, binding. Treat as a spell.
- **Summon Enforcement** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, summon a patrol, guard unit, or templar attendant to the scene. They arrive within one beat and obey you for one meaningful action.
- **Writ of Charges** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, accuse someone of a crime against the city-state. The accusation itself creates a Bain against them for the rest of the scene, whether or not it's true. This Play cannot be defiled.
- **The King's Attention** _(rare, once per session)_ — Once per session, call directly on the king's power for something impossible at your rank — banish a rival, commandeer a caravan, invoke the king's name to stop a mob. The cost is owed. The king will collect.

### How Defile & Preserve modify these Plays

The Templar's channel to their king is a **pipe**, not a source. Most of the time it works one way: preserved, controlled, with no local evidence.

- **Preserved:** The king's power flows through you cleanly. No blight. No trace. This is the default, and it's what makes templars so terrifying — they cast without showing it.
- **Defiled:** You draw more than the king has allotted you. Either the local area pays (fear, withering, unnatural cold, plants dying in a ring at your feet as the overflow vents) _or_ the king notices. If the king notices, the next time you use **The King's Attention**, the price is higher — and the king decides what higher means.

A loyal Templar is never publicly called a defiler; they're a _templar_, an agent of the state. A Templar who loses the king's favor and keeps casting — with the channel closing — has to start drawing from plants or sacrifice instead, and becomes a defiler in the street's eyes immediately.

## Path: Illusionist

**Path Family:** Rogue **Role:** Performer, con-artist, stage magician, spy. You survive by making people see what isn't there — and you use no magic at all. **Core Fantasy:** Everyone thinks you're a defiler. You're not. You don't cast anything. You're just _better at lying than they are at seeing._ And if a templar ever figures that out, they'll laugh and let you go — or kill you for the trick of it. **Energy Source:** None. You are not a caster. You never defile, never preserve, never draw. Everything you do is sleight of hand, misdirection, props, psychology, and theater.

### Proficiency Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **I trained in the performer's craft.** I know showmanship, timing, voice, body, and holding a crowd.
- **I mastered sleight of hand and close-up deception.** I know palming, switching, lifting, and making small things disappear.
- **I studied the mimicry arts.** I know voices, faces, mannerisms — enough to pass as someone else until a close friend looks twice.
- **I learned to read people.** I know microexpressions, tells, motive, and what someone _wants_ to believe.

### Specialty Options (choose 2 of 4)

- **Signature Deception** — pick one: stage work, close-up magic, impersonation, con-artistry. +1 in that domain.
- **Sleight of Hand** — +1 on close-up deception — palming, switching, lifting.
- **Impersonation** — +1 when passing as someone else in voice, manner, or appearance.
- **Reading the Mark** — +1 when identifying what a target _wants_ to see, hear, or believe.

### Plays (all 5)

- **Nothing Here to See** _(signature)_ — You and your immediate effects default to unremarkable. In any crowd, public space, or busy scene, you are not noticed unless someone is specifically looking for you or you act overtly. You can be seen — you just aren't registered.
- **Cast a Figure** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, stage the appearance of a person, creature, or object using props, shadow, mirrors, a confederate, a voice-throw, a costume change, or pure patter. It holds up to one meaningful moment of scrutiny — a guard waving through, a distraction drawing attention, a mark following a false trail — and then falls apart. Close examination reveals the trick.
- **Whisper in the Ear** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, plant a sound, voice, or suggestion in a specific target's ear — a thrown voice, a whispered lie, a staged overheard conversation. They hear it as real. What they do with it is up to them.
- **False Face** _(scene)_ — Once per scene, shift your appearance using paint, posture, costume, and voice to match someone you've observed. Holds for the scene unless directly challenged (a pat-down, a password, a trusted friend).
- **The Great Deception** _(rare, once per session)_ — Once per session, stage a con of scene-altering scale — a false army at the gates (hired extras, smoke, drums), a templar who isn't there (a double, stolen livery), a corpse that isn't dead (drugs, a switch, a prepared wound). Work out the setup with the Null in advance; execution becomes a single roll with three Edges.

### Note on the Illusionist

Because the Illusionist is non-magical, they face a unique campaign dynamic: **the street thinks they're a defiler, but they aren't.** A templar who witnesses an Illusionist's work will initially assume magic. If the Illusionist is caught or investigated, revealing the _trick_ might save them — or might get them killed for the affront.

This makes the Illusionist especially interesting for Dark Sun: they inhabit the social space of a defiler without the actual risk of _being_ one, and they can use that misperception as an asset (scare people who don't know better) or a liability (get lynched by a mob that thinks they saw real magic).

# Magic in Athas

## The Core Truth

Magic on Athas is **the act of drawing life energy from a source and shaping it into an effect.** That is all magic is — cleric, druid, templar, or wizard. The chants, the symbols, the rites, the writs — those are trappings. The real substance is: _something alive gives up part of itself, and something happens because of it._

Every caster on Athas faces three questions whenever they use magic:

1. **Where does the energy come from?** (the _source_)
2. **Do I take it gently or rip it out?** (_preserve_ or _defile_)
3. **What does that leave behind?** (the _cost_)

The answers are not set at character creation. They are chosen every time a Play is used. This is what makes magic on Athas a living moral problem instead of a character-sheet option.

## The Two Methods

### Preserve

You draw the energy slowly, taking only what the source can replace. The ground holds. The plants live. The animal wakes with nothing worse than a strange dream.

- **Requires a beat of setup.** You cannot preserve-cast as a reflex. The Null will tell you how long — a held breath in a calm scene, a full action in combat, longer for big effects.
- **Requires a living source within reach.** What "within reach" means depends on your path — plants in a grove, a breeze if you're air-attuned, a spring if you're water-attuned.
- **Leaves no trace.** Templars, mobs, and other casters cannot tell you cast unless they saw you do it with their own eyes.
- **Resolves your Play normally.** No bonus, no blight, no reveal.

### Defile

You rip the energy out all at once. The land dies in a ring around you. The plants turn to ash. The animal drops mid-step. The spring runs silt.

- **Casts immediately.** No setup beat.
- **Gains one Edge** on the Play automatically.
- **Works in dead land.** Ruins, ash-fields, cities — because you'll take whatever life is there, including things you hadn't planned to.
- **Leaves a visible wound.** A ring of dead ground scaled to the Play. Visible for days. Readable by anyone who knows what defiling looks like — which on Athas is everyone.
- **Marks you.** In the next scene in any settled place, anyone looking for defilers has an Edge against you. Templars will investigate. Mobs will form.

### The Temptation

Defile is always _tempting._ It is faster, more powerful, and works when preserve cannot. A preserver with no living source nearby has to watch their ally die or defile to save them. A cleric whose element has abandoned them can only defile — or do nothing. The game's most interesting moments are the ones where preserving is technically possible but costly, and defiling is technically easy but scarring.

The Null should never make defiling a free choice. There is always a _seen_ cost — someone watches, the blight is obvious, the next city notices. Over a campaign, a character who habitually defiles becomes known, hunted, changed. Over a career, they become what they feared.

## Sources of Energy

What a caster can draw from is determined by their path. This is the main mechanical difference between the magical paths; the Plays themselves are just the shape of what they do with the energy.

|Source|Used by|Notes|
|---|---|---|
|**Plants**|Arcanist, Druid|The default living source. Abundant in oases, groves, the Forest Ridge. Absent in the deep waste and inside city walls.|
|**Your chosen element**|Cleric|Not a living source. You draw from air, fire, water, or earth where it exists in meaningful form. You cannot draw from life at all.|
|**Your sorcerer-king's reserve**|Templar|The king holds power drawn from life elsewhere, long ago. You channel a fragment of it. Works anywhere in the king's domain. Closes if the king is displeased.|
|**Animals**|Arcanists with training|Not available by default. Requires a specific proficiency or Play. Drawing from animals is more efficient than from plants but carries a heavy social penalty — killing a caravan's kanks is not subtle.|
|**Sentient beings**|Sorcerer-kings, villains, fallen PCs|Player-accessible only through specific Plays or extreme Self-Sacrifice of others. Leaves a corpse. Politically catastrophic. This is the road to becoming what the world fears.|

A caster can never draw from a source their path does not grant them. A cleric cannot defile a grove; they have no way to reach that energy. A wizard cannot draw from fire alone; without a Cleric's attunement, fire is just heat.

## Self-Sacrifice

Any caster, any path, any scene. This is not a Play — it is a rule of the magic system.

When a caster cannot reach their normal source — dead land with no plants, an element absent or hostile, a templar whose king has closed the channel — they may draw from _themselves._ The body has life energy. The mind has it. A caster can spend their own.

### Minor Self-Sacrifice

You pour more of yourself into the casting than is safe. You take a **-2 Bain** for the rest of the scene: exhaustion, nosebleed, trembling hands, cold sweat, tunnel vision. Your Play resolves as preserved — no bonus, no blight, no reveal. You look pale and spent afterward.

### Major Self-Sacrifice

You push past what your body can spare. The Null sets a permanent cost. Examples:

- A year of your life span.
- A chunk of your long-term health — a limp, a tremor, weakened lungs.
- A dulling of your mind — lost memories, slower thought.
- Grey in your hair, or white, all at once.
- A piece of your soul — you no longer laugh the same way, or love the same way, or feel fear the same way.

In exchange, your Play **either casts as a Rare Play outside your once-per-session limit, or resolves with two extra Edges.** Your choice.

This cost is narrative, not tracked. The Null remembers. The character carries it. If the same player self-sacrifices repeatedly across sessions, they visibly age, fray, hollow. Eventually they do not come back.

### The Breaking Point

Repeated Major Self-Sacrifice in one session begins to kill you. The Null tracks this fictionally, not with numbers. If the Null tells you that you are "one more away from not standing up," they mean it — and the next Major Self-Sacrifice is your last cast. Heroes die this way on Athas. It is one of the holiest things a caster can do, and the world will remember.

Self-Sacrifice is how magic stays alive when the world has nothing left to give. It is the caster's answer to being in a dead place with no choices — and it is the one method of magic Athas itself respects.

## Public Perception vs. Mechanical Method

**A caster's public standing is separate from how they actually cast.**

Athas does not grade magic by technique. Athas grades magic by _who vouches for you._ If you have a writ, a temple, a grove, a sorcerer-king's livery — you are a priest, a druid, a templar. If you do not — whatever you are, to the street, you are a **defiler.**

The consequence: a preserver-trained Arcanist casting carefully in an alley is in exactly as much danger as a reckless defiler in the same alley. The mob does not know the difference, and would not care if they did. Only other casters and the most educated templars can tell the two apart, and most of those have reasons not to.

Conversely, a Templar who defiles openly, channeling their king's power to kill a rebel, is not publicly called a defiler. They are a _templar performing their duty._ The same act, the same blight, the same cost — different name on the tongue of the crowd.

This cultural layer matters because it means:

- **Leaving a temple, grove, or templarate is catastrophic.** You don't just lose a Play; you lose your public standing, and become a hunted defiler overnight.
- **Joining one is valuable.** An unaligned arcanist who finds a grove that will shelter her, or a temple willing to claim her, gains a layer of protection no spell can provide.
- **Being recognized is everything.** A templar out of uniform in a foreign city is, mechanically, just a caster. Their "By the King's Will" Play works only where the king's authority is acknowledged.

The Null should lean into this. When a cleric's temple falls, the cleric is a defiler by next dawn. When a rebel druid's grove is burned, her bond breaks and so does her cover. When a templar's king dies — well, then nobody knows what she is, and neither does she.

## What This Looks Like at the Table

When a player activates a magical Play:

1. The player declares the Play and what they want it to do.
2. The Null asks: **preserve or defile?** (Unless the Play cannot be defiled — those are marked in each path.)
3. If **preserve**: confirm a living source is within reach; take the setup beat; resolve the Play normally.
4. If **defile**: resolve immediately with one extra Edge; describe the blight; note the mark on the character for the next scene in a settled place.
5. If **self-sacrifice**: decide minor or major; take the Bain or the permanent cost; resolve as preserved (minor) or with two extra Edges or out-of-slot Rare (major).
6. The Null describes what the world saw, who noticed, and what that costs.

The choice of method is a _conversation at the table,_ not a dice roll. The interesting part of magic in Athas is the ethics of the choice, not the math of the resolution.

## Design Notes for the Null

**Preserve should feel like restraint, not weakness.** A preserver who plans ahead, sets up their cast, and uses the pause well should win dramatic moments regularly. If players feel that preserving is always worse than defiling, the balance is wrong — tighten the fictional consequences of defiling.

**Defile should always hurt someone, even if not the caster.** If a player defiles and nothing visible happens, the world has failed to be Athas. A grove dies. A farmer weeps. A caravan's kanks sicken. Someone notices.

**Self-Sacrifice is a hero's move, not a crutch.** If a player uses it casually, make the permanent costs land. A character who self-sacrifices twice should look different. If they do it four times in a campaign, they should die spectacularly and be remembered.

**Sentient defiling is campaign-ending.** If a player defiles a person directly, the story changes. Templars hunt them. Their allies may walk away. The Null should treat this as a plot event, not a mechanic — the character has crossed into villain territory, and the campaign will either end in their death or their transformation.

**The public/mechanical split is the single richest vein in the whole system.** Let NPCs misidentify casters. Let preservers be burned for crimes they didn't commit. Let defilers walk free because they wear the right robes. This is Dark Sun.

## The Scale of the Cast

Not every spell takes the same energy. A caster lighting a candle draws almost nothing. A caster leveling a building draws enough life to kill a grove. The Null and the player work out the scale together before the cast resolves.

NullFrame magic uses three tiers of scale, tied to the Play rhythm:

### Cantrip Scale — _small, incidental, free_

Matches: **Signature** Plays, Cantrip-tier effects, minor shaping.

- **Source needed:** A single living thing. A handful of grass. A weed. A breath of wind. A handful of embers. A scoop from a waterskin. Your own warmth for a moment.
- **Preserve time:** A held breath. Half a beat. Essentially no delay.
- **Defile trace:** A patch of grass turns grey. A single leaf curls. The nearest flame dims. Easy to miss.
- **Examples:** Cantrip Work. Lighting a torch from across a room. Hearing a whisper from behind a door. A tremor in a cup.

### Scene Scale — _meaningful, visible, shaped_

Matches: **Scene** Plays, combat spells, deliberate effects.

- **Source needed:** A meaningful patch of living matter. A bush. A small tree. A creature the size of a dog. A full campfire. A flowing stream. A fresh corpse's residual warmth. A full lungful of wind.
- **Preserve time:** A beat of setup. A full action. A moment of visible focus in which the caster is vulnerable.
- **Defile trace:** A ring of dead earth five paces across. A tree reduced to ash. A spring running brown for the scene. The nearest people's skin goes cold.
- **Examples:** Cast the Flame. Channel the King. Call the Element. Shape of the Beast.

### Rare Scale — _scene-altering, catastrophic, memorable_

Matches: **Rare** Plays, session-shaping effects.

- **Source needed:** A grove. A caravan's worth of animals. A field of crops. A sustained storm. A flowing river. An entire hearth-fire fed for hours. Or — for the particularly dark — a living person.
- **Preserve time:** Minutes, at minimum. Often longer. Usually a ritual, a song, or a pause in which the caster must not be interrupted. The Null sets the time to match the fiction.
- **Defile trace:** A hundred paces of land turned to grey dust. A grove reduced to white skeletons. A river running dry for a season. A spring permanently lost. Every living thing within the radius that wasn't a PC takes a Bain or dies.
- **Examples:** Unmaking. Wrath of the Element. Wrath of the Land. The King's Attention.

### Matching the Scale

The Null sets the scale based on what the player is trying to do, not on which Play they named. A Signature Play used for an enormous effect scales up. A Rare Play used for a small task scales down. The three tiers are anchors, not cages.

**Quick check for the Null:**

- _Could a bystander have done it with mundane effort?_ → Cantrip scale.
- _Could a squad of soldiers do it in ten minutes of hard work?_ → Scene scale.
- _Could nothing mundane do it at all?_ → Rare scale.

### Scale and the Living Source

The source has to be _proportional_ to the scale. Trying to cast a Scene-scale spell with only a single blade of grass available?

- **If preserving:** It cannot be done. You do not have enough to draw from. The Play fails before it starts. You may instead try Self-Sacrifice, or downgrade to a Cantrip-scale effect.
- **If defiling:** The ring of devastation _extends outward until it has taken enough._ If there is only one weed nearby, the defile-ring widens searching for life. It may take the thing you were standing near. It may take your companion's horse. It may take, if nothing else is alive, a piece of _you._

This is a central Athasian truth: **defiling in dead land is dangerous because the energy comes from somewhere, and you may not get to choose where.**

### Scale and Setup Time

Preserved casting is slower than defiled casting at every tier, but the gap _widens_ with scale:

|Scale|Preserve time|Defile time|
|---|---|---|
|Cantrip|Half a beat|Instant|
|Scene|A full action|Instant|
|Rare|Minutes or more, usually a ritual|One action|

This is why defiling is tempting in combat at Scene scale and nearly irresistible at Rare scale. It is also why a preserved Rare-scale spell is a genuinely heroic undertaking — the caster has to be protected for the duration, and every enemy in the area knows something big is coming.

### Scale and Self-Sacrifice

Self-Sacrifice from your own body follows the same scale rules:

- **Cantrip from self:** A tired flicker. Barely worth noting. No Bain required.
- **Scene from self:** Minor Self-Sacrifice — you take a -2 Bain for the scene.
- **Rare from self:** Major Self-Sacrifice — the Null sets a permanent cost.

A caster in dead land attempting a Rare-scale effect from their own body alone is doing something most casters never survive.
